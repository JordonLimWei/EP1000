Laser Cutting Certificate


Music Box

Before looking at how I laser cut this music box, have a look at how I made the 3D model in Fusion 360 [here].
Before I can export the 3D model into .dxf format, I have to lay flat all the parts of the music box. Thanks to this video, I learnt a new feature of Fusion 360.
Go to the manufacturing workspace> additive> create manufacturing model> right-click the manufacturing model> edit manufacturing model> modify> arrange.
Export as DXF
Right-click edit manufacturing model allows me to save the layflat sketch as a dxf file.
Import into Illustrator
After importing into Illustrator, I check to see if the file is to scale such that it will be laser cut to size.

Machine and Settings

Machine Universal VLS6.60
Work Area(mm) 813x457
Material Plywood
Material Thickness 3mm
Cut Settings
  • Power: 40%
  • Speed: 16%
Engrave Settings
  • Power: 20%
  • Speed: 70%

Final Product


After cutting out the parts, I have to use a hand mill to drill out the hole for the handle of the music instrument and the end result came out pretty well.
